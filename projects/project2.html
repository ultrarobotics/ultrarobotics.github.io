Hobby Quadruped Project

This project was hobby project from the end of my Bachelor's to the start of my Master's. I developed a torque-controlled quadruped robot from scratch, gaining hands-on experience in mechanical design, electrical integration, and software development.

  • Mechanical Design:
    • Selected components like bearings, belts, motors, and motor drivers to meet constraints and requirements.
    • Designed lightweight, backdriveable gearboxes using a double-stage belt reduction.
    • Addressed challenges like cable management and space constraints for the robot's electronics.
    • Used SolidWorks for a full CAD model of the robot.
    • Fabricated components using a Creality CR-10 3D printer and tested prototypes for durability.
  • Electrical Integration:
    • Experimented with ST's motor driver developer kit and SimpleFOC before choosing for an out-of-the-box solution.
    • Integrated ODrives with AMT encoders and controlled them via Raspberry Pi 4 with a CAN shield.
    • Designed the circuit with safety measures such as inrush current protection and ground loop prevention.
