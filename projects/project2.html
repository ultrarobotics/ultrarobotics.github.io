Hobby Quadruped Project

Duration: February 2021 – July 2023

Through this project, I built a torque-controlled quadruped from scratch, gaining hands-on experience in mechanical design, electrical integration, and software development. This experience not only deepened my understanding of legged locomotion but also laid the foundation for my later research in the co-design of legged robots.

Hardware Development: Designing and Building the Robot
  • Defined platform requirements, focusing on backdriveability, modularity, and cost-efficiency.
  • Designed a double-stage belt reduction gearbox to minimize backlash and reduce weight, integrating the gearbox seamlessly into each subcomponent of the robot.
  • Worked out the mechanical design in SolidWorks.
  • Fabricated parts using a Creality CR-10 3D printer and iterated designs based on durability and manufacturability testing.
  • Addressed key design challenges such as cable management and spatial constraints for the embedded electronics.
Electrical System: Integration & Power Management
  • Explored different motor driver solutions, testing ST’s developer kit and SimpleFOC.
  • Integrated ODrive motor controllers with AMT encoders for precise torque control.
  • Designed and implemented a safe and robust electrical system, incorporating inrush current protection and ground loop prevention.
  • Established a CAN-based communication protocol between the computer and motor controllers for efficient real-time control.
Software Development: Control and Simulation
  • Converted the CAD model from SolidWorks → URDF → MJCF for use in simulation environments.
  • Developed an inverse kinematics solver and trajectory generation algorithms in C++ to control leg movements.
  • Built a custom ROS 2 stack for high-level robot control running on the Raspberry Pi 4.
Results & Insights
  • Successfully created a working, compliant quadruped robot capable of basic walking.
